The Question

Comparable companies valuation — automated and scalable:

  • 💼 Regression: Given a company’s revenue, profit, assets, industry, and margins — what EV/Revenue multiple does the market assign? (Implied enterprise value for any company, public or private)
  • 🎯 Classification: Is this company trading below its industry’s median multiple? (Undervaluation screen)

Dataset: Forbes Global 2000, 2026 edition — 1,901 public companies across 27 industries (after removing EV/Revenue outliers). Used as a comps universe — the same methodology investment banks use to value acquisition targets.

Why It Matters

Regression — Revenue Multiple

  • Private equity and corporate buyers need to value private targets
  • No public price → use comparable public companies
  • Model output: “A company with this profile trades at ~2.5× revenue”
  • Multiply by target’s revenue → implied enterprise value range

Classification — Undervaluation Screen

  • Which Forbes Global 2000 companies trade below their industry peers?
  • These are the cheapest acquisitions on an EV/Revenue basis
  • Model output: probability that fundamentals imply a below-median multiple
  • First-pass acquisition screen before detailed diligence

The Data at a Glance

  • 1,901 rows, 27 industries — same population app.py trains on live
  • Classification balanced by construction: threshold at industry median

The Industry Comps Table

Semiconductors (~17×) and Software (~5.5×) command the richest multiples; Food Markets, Trading, and Insurance trade near or below 1×. Industry is the most important M&A valuation input after margin quality.

What We Built: The Pipeline

Important

No leakage: MarketValue_B/net_debt_B compute the target and are excluded from features. Scaler, one-hot encoder, and RFE selector all fit inside the Pipeline on the training split only.

The Model Ladder

Four rungs per task — simple to complex:

Rung Regression (EV/Rev ×) Classification (Undervaluation)
1. Baseline DummyRegressor (mean) DummyClassifier
2. Linear Ridge Regression Logistic Regression
3. Tree Decision Tree Decision Tree
4. Ensemble XGBoost XGBoost

Every rung runs through the same RFE-selected feature set (top half of transformed columns) — a fair, apples-to-apples comparison.

Results — Regression (Test Set)

                 R²  MAE (log)  RMSE (log)
Model                                     
Dummy (Mean)  -0.00       0.67        0.90
Ridge          0.30       0.53        0.75
Decision Tree  0.16       0.58        0.82
XGBoost        0.35       0.47        0.72

Target is log1p(EV/Revenue) — reduces the right-skew that a raw multiple has. XGBoost’s R² gain over Ridge here is real but modest: industry and margin already capture most of the signal a linear model can extract.

What Drives the Multiple?

Industry and scale dominate. RFE eliminates the raw Sales_B/Assets_B columns in favour of their log-transformed versions — markets pay premiums for industry exposure and margin quality, the same factors M&A buyers pay up for.

Live App

Forbes Global 2000 Comparable Companies Tool — run locally today via streamlit run app.py

  • Enter: any company’s revenue, profit, assets, industry, or search by ticker for a live Yahoo Finance lookup — or snapshot a manual entry as a loaded company, for private/unlisted firms
  • Get: predicted EV/Revenue multiple + implied enterprise value + an undervalued/overvalued/fair-value verdict for public companies (blended 30% model / 70% peer median) — or a plain estimated valuation, with no market to compare against, for private/manual entries
  • Shows: industry comps table + the 20 closest comparable companies, refined by business-description similarity; downloadable as a full Excel report

Honest Takeaways

  1. Industry is the dominant M&A valuation variable. Semiconductors and Software command 5-17× what Food Markets or Insurance do at the same revenue.
  2. Profit margin and scale predict who gets premium multiples within an industry — RFE keeps the log-transformed size features and drops their raw duplicates.
  3. XGBoost beats linear models on both tasks, but the regression gain is modest (R² 0.25 vs. Ridge’s 0.21) — most of the signal is already linear once industry is one-hot encoded.
  4. Use for directional screening, not binding valuation. EV/Revenue is trained in log-space precisely because the raw multiple is too skewed for a single RMSE number to be trustworthy on its own.
  5. This is a 2026 single-year snapshot. Rate environment, sector rotation, and macro conditions shift multiples substantially — retrain periodically.
